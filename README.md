## 👥 Population vs. Sample

| Concept [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] | Definition | Key Characteristic |
|---|---|---|
| Population | The entire group of individuals or objects you want to study. | Complete data source |
| Sample | A smaller, manageable subset of the population collected for analysis. | Representative data slice |

## 🧮 What is Statistics?
Statistics is a framework of mathematical methods and tools that enables us to answer important questions about data. It is divided into two primary branches: [6, 7, 8] 
## 1. 📈 Descriptive Statistics
Transforms raw observations into meaningful, easy-to-interpret information. [9, 10] 

* Measures of Central Tendency: Mean, Median, Mode
* Measures of Variability: Range, Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD), Variance, Standard Deviation
* Measures of Shape: Skewness
* Percentiles: Quartiles, Percentiles, Boxplots
* Frequency Distribution: Histograms, Plots, etc.
* Relationship Metrics: Covariance, Correlation [11, 12, 13] 

## 2. 🔮 Inferential Statistics
Uses data from a small sample to draw conclusions about the entire population. [14, 15] 

* Core Theories: Central Limit Theorem
* Hypothesis Testing: Z-test, T-test, Chi-Square Test
* Foundations: Probability Theory [16, 17, 18, 19, 20]
